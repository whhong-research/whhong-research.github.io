Offshore Wind Turbine Condition Monitoring and Maintenance Strategy Optimization: A Cost-Effectiveness Based Decision Framework

Mar 03, 2025
简体中文

Article Information: This article is a reading note of the paper “A generic framework for the optimisation of maintenance strategies for offshore wind turbines”. This study constructs a generic framework for optimizing maintenance strategies for offshore wind turbines, with particular focus on the synergistic application of SCADA systems and dedicated Condition Monitoring Systems (CMS).

Research Background and Core Questions

Research Objectives

This paper aims to address two key questions in offshore wind turbine operation and maintenance:

  1. Feasibility of Monitoring Systems: Can SCADA systems in wind farms serve as a viable alternative to dedicated Condition Monitoring Systems (CMS)?

  2. Integrated Optimization Strategy: How can CMS be combined with O&M strategies to maximize the financial benefits of Offshore Wind Turbines (OWTs)?

Core Contributions

This research establishes a comprehensive simulation framework capable of:

  • Simulating the degradation process of wind turbine components
  • Evaluating the detection capabilities of different monitoring systems
  • Optimizing the cost-effectiveness ratio of maintenance strategies

Basic Model Assumptions

Health State Classification

The research divides the health state of each subsystem into four levels:

State Level Description O&M Impact
Normal No faults Normal operation
Minor Fault Minor defects exist Allowed to continue operation
Critical Fault Serious defects exist Can still operate, but needs attention
Failure Complete failure Triggers shutdown

Key Assumptions:

  • In minor fault and critical fault states, the subsystem is still allowed to operate
  • Critical faults are more easily detected by CMS
  • Different components have different false alarm rates at different stages of fault development
  • Any subsystem failure will trigger wind turbine shutdown

Degradation Process Model

  • The degradation process of each subsystem follows the Weibull Distribution
  • Parameters of the Weibull Distribution are derived from publicly available failure rate data in the literature

Other Assumptions

Parameter Setting Description
Wind Turbine Service Life 20 years Standard design life
Post-repair State “as good as new” Complete repair assumption

O&M Strategy System

1. Corrective Maintenance

Definition: Maintenance performed after a substructure has completely failed.

Characteristics:

  • Passive responsive maintenance
  • Usually highest cost (including downtime losses and emergency repair costs)
  • Suitable for non-critical components or situations where failure consequences are not serious

2. Time-Based Maintenance

Definition: Maintenance performed according to predetermined time intervals.

Classification:

  • Basic Service (BS): Discover and fix common, easy-to-repair but difficult-to-monitor problems
  • Advanced Service (AS): Comprehensive inspection of all structural components

Optimization Direction: Shortening the time interval between adjacent AS can improve wind turbine availability, but is still not as effective as using a monitoring system.

3. Condition-Based Maintenance (CBM)

Definition: Maintenance based on the actual health state of the wind turbine.

Core Elements:

  • Relies on CMS fault detection capability
  • Real-time assessment of equipment health status
  • Intervention before failure occurs

Effectiveness: Depends on CMS detection accuracy, response speed, and timeliness of maintenance decisions.

Maintenance Framework System

The research defines three maintenance levels:

Maintenance Level Corresponding Fault Type Maintenance Content
Maintenance 1 Minor Fault Repair minor defects, prevent deterioration
Maintenance 2 Critical Fault Repair serious defects, restore function
Maintenance 3 Complete Failure Comprehensive repair or component replacement

Simulation Summary

  1. Parameter Sampling: Sample all random variables to generate parameters
  2. Single Turbine Simulation: Execute all above processes for one turbine until reaching service life
  3. Iterative Convergence: Repeat iterations until convergence (converges to stable values after 30,000 iterations, all simulations performed 50,000 iterations)

Simulation Results and Performance Indicators

Wind Turbine Availability

Key Findings

  • Shortening AS Intervals: Shortening adjacent Advanced Service (AS) time intervals can improve wind turbine availability, but is still not as effective as using any monitoring system
  • Monitoring System Advantages: Using SCADA or CMS monitoring systems can significantly improve availability
  • Dual System Effects: When performing frequent AS, combining two monitoring systems may lead to decreased availability, because the downtime for repairing the turbine after damage detection becomes longer

Research Conclusions and Practical Implications

Main Conclusions

  1. Value of Monitoring Systems: Both dedicated CMS and SCADA systems can significantly improve wind turbine availability, but SCADA, as a lower-cost alternative, has considerable economic benefits

  2. Maintenance Strategy Optimization: Condition-Based Maintenance (CBM) combined with appropriate monitoring systems can maximize availability while controlling costs

  3. System Synergy Effects: The synergistic use of SCADA and CMS may produce diminishing marginal returns in some cases, requiring trade-offs between additional detection capabilities and downtime costs

  4. Cost-Effectiveness Balance: COA indicators show that the optimal strategy is not always the most advanced monitoring system, but rather the best balance point between cost and benefit

Engineering Practice Recommendations

Application Scenario Recommended Strategy Rationale
New Wind Farm CMS + CBM Maximizing lifecycle benefits
Existing Wind Farm Retrofit SCADA Upgrade + Targeted CMS Balancing cost control and benefits
Budget-Constrained Scenarios Optimized SCADA Algorithms + Periodic Maintenance Minimal input for considerable returns
High-Risk Environments Complete CMS + Dual Monitoring Ensuring reliability and safety

Future Research Directions

  1. Machine Learning Integration: Incorporating AI algorithms into SCADA data analysis to improve fault prediction accuracy
  2. Digital Twin Applications: Building digital twin models for wind turbines to achieve real-time status simulation and prediction
  3. Multi-Wind Farm Collaboration: Researching wind farm-level centralized monitoring and maintenance resource optimization scheduling
  4. Full Lifecycle Cost Models: Establishing more comprehensive LCOE and maintenance cost correlation models
