Paper Review: Multi-Objective Maintenance Strategy Optimization for Offshore Wind Farms Considering Uncertainty

Li, M., Jiang, X., Carroll, J., & Negenborn, R. R. (2022). A multi-objective maintenance strategy optimization framework for offshore wind farms considering uncertainty. Applied Energy, 321, 119284. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apenergy.2022.119284

Research Background

Offshore wind farm operation and maintenance (O&M) faces unique challenges. Research indicates that O&M costs account for 14%-30% of total life cycle costs (LCC), while the offshore environment significantly reduces turbine availability—offshore wind turbine availability may drop to as low as 60%-70%. Therefore, developing efficient O&M strategies is crucial for reducing offshore wind energy costs.

Literature Review

Traditional Maintenance Strategies

The wind energy industry currently employs two main traditional maintenance strategies:

Strategy Type Description
Corrective Maintenance Repairs performed only after turbine failure
Preventive Maintenance Scheduled maintenance conducted at fixed intervals

These strategies remain mainstream in the industry but suffer from efficiency limitations.

Opportunistic Maintenance

Core Concept: Performing maintenance on one component creates an “opportunity window” that can be leveraged to simultaneously repair other qualified components within the same wind farm.

Characteristics of Existing Models:

  • Decision variables are typically set as maintenance thresholds related to component reliability or mean time to failure
  • Research Gap 1: Existing models do not connect maintenance thresholds with actual component condition
    • Condition monitoring techniques can collect diagnostic signals reflecting key component structural health
    • Remaining useful life prediction models can assist decision-makers in performing predictive maintenance based on future component states

Uncertainty Factors

Offshore wind O&M faces multiple uncertainties:

Uncertainty Sources in Life Cycle O&M Framework:

  • Statistical uncertainty in component reliability estimation
  • Maintenance accessibility issues due to weather variability
  • Insufficient maintenance resources

Limitations of Existing Research (Research Gap 2):

  • Only study the impact of one or two types of uncertainty on maintenance performance
  • Do not involve optimization problems
  • Do not consider novel maintenance strategies
  • Do not adequately demonstrate the impact of uncertainty on maintenance performance

Uncertainties Considered in This Paper:

  • Randomness of failure times
  • Deviation between true and actual failure times
  • Uncertain maintenance consequences

Uncertainties Not Considered:

  • Accessibility affected by weather factors
  • Vessel and spare part availability

Basic Components of Maintenance Models

Typical maintenance models include the following elements:

  • Degradation Model: Describes the system performance degradation process over time
  • State Information: Describes available information about the current system state
  • Maintenance Actions: Possible O&M measures that can be taken
  • Maintenance Consequences: Expected outcomes of maintenance actions

O&M Optimization Objectives

O&M objectives typically include: reliability, safety, life cycle cost, carbon emissions, etc.

Optimization Objectives Considered in This Paper:

  • Minimize maintenance costs (costs related to maintenance effort)
  • Minimize energy losses (production losses caused by downtime)

Maintenance Model

Basic Assumptions

  1. All wind turbines are of the same type, simplified as a collection of multiple key components; failure of any component leads to complete system failure
  2. Once O&M decisions are made, maintenance activities can be implemented smoothly
  3. Temporal fluctuations in material costs, vessel chartering, and labor-related costs are not considered

Failure Modeling

Weibull Distribution Modeling: A two-parameter Weibull distribution is used to simulate the failure probability density function:

Random Shock Modeling:

  • In addition to degradation failures, random damage is considered (unexpected events such as lightning strikes, hurricanes, etc.)
  • Non-homogeneous Poisson process is used to model random shocks
  • Given an intensity function λ, the inverse distribution method is used to generate shock occurrence times

Environmental Impact Classification: Environmental impacts are divided into three levels:

Level Impact Description
Critical Component fails directly
Influential Accelerates component degradation, causing abrupt increase in equivalent age $b_m$
Minor Only temporarily affects turbine operation; turbine returns to normal operational state after a short period

Maintenance Opportunities and Component Condition

Three Types of Maintenance Opportunities:

Opportunity Type Trigger Condition
Failure-based Opportunity A component in the wind turbine experiences degradation failure, causing turbine shutdown
Incident-based Opportunity The wind turbine suffers a sudden critical environmental impact
Ageing-based Opportunity No failure occurs in the farm, but a certain percentage of components reach a specific degree of ageing

Maintenance Strategy Determination Framework:

Maintenance Categories

Four types of maintenance operations are considered:

Maintenance Type Description
Failure Replacement Replacement after complete component failure
Preventive Replacement Replacement before component failure
Major Repair Significant restoration of components
Basic Maintenance Routine maintenance and minor repairs

Summary

The multi-objective maintenance strategy optimization framework proposed in this paper has the following characteristics:

  1. Comprehensive Uncertainty Consideration: Simultaneously considers failure time randomness, observation errors, and maintenance consequence uncertainty
  2. Multi-Opportunity Trigger Mechanism: Establishes a unified framework for three types of maintenance opportunities: failure-based, incident-based, and ageing-based
  3. Multi-Objective Optimization: Balances two competing objectives of maintenance cost and energy loss
  4. State-Associated Decision Making: Combines maintenance thresholds with actual component conditions

This framework provides theoretical foundation and decision support tools for economically efficient O&M of offshore wind farms.
